CULTURAL SIGNIFICANCE

Al Bayt Stadium is a football stadium in Al Khor, intended to be used for matches in the 2022 FIFA World Cup.

The concept of Al Bayt Stadium is inspired by the traditional Bedouin tent, which is the home of nomadic people who have etched a living from Qatar's deserts for millennia. It is called Bayt Al Sharâ€™ar, from which the stadium also takes its name. In original tents, the outside is decorated with stripes, which is a way of signaling which tribe or family the tent belongs to. And the Al Baty Stadium is dominated by white color, along with strongly contrasting black stripes. Furthermore, the inside decoration is covered by the vibrant sadu patterns. Visitors to Qatar will get a taste of traditional design from the region, while local fans will be reminded of their proud heritage.

As well as being incredibly eye-catching, the tent design is deeply practical. The shade provided by the tent structure and its retractable roof system will complement the stadium's cooling technologies, helping maintain a comfortable temperature inside without using any extra power.

SUSTAINABILITY

The stadium, surrounded by impressively landscaped plazas and parking sites, will not only meet all FIFA criteria, but also environmental and sustainability guidelines set out by LEED and GSAS standards.

The current capacity stands at 60,000 to allow hosting one semi-final game in 2022. In turn, the entire upper tier will be removed in legacy mode, leaving only 32,000 seats. And the seats will be donated to developing nations across the world.

With the design of the tent structure, the lightweight canopies can stretch out towards the pitch from every side, providing plenty of shade to supplement the cooling technologies. In this way, the stadium can minimize the energy amount required to keep football fans and players cool and host the matches at moderate temperatures even in summer heat.
