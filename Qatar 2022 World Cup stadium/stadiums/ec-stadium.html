CULTURAL SIGNIFICANCE

Education City Stadium is a 40,000-seat football stadium located in Al Rayyan. It is located within several university campuses at the Qatar Foundation's Education City.

The Stadium has a design that draws on the rich history of Islamic architecture, blending it with striking modernity. The facade features triangles that form complex, diamond-esque geometrical patterns, appearing to change color with the sun's movement across the sky. The enclosed outer shell is shaped to resemble a diamond, highly reflective during the day and illuminated with thousands of diodes in the night. With partially perforated cladding it will also provide sufficient control of temperatures inside, enabling to maintain 24-28 degrees no matter the weather.

Like diamonds, the stadium's design represents quality, durability, and resilience, and will become something to be treasured in the times to come.

SUSTAINABILITY

In January 2019, the stadium received a 5-star design and build rating from the Global Sustainability Assessment System (GSAS).

The stadium is equipped with photovoltaic panels, which are embedded into the cladding, providing up to 20% of matchday power demand. Even after the tournament is over, the stadium will remain a focal point of the community long. Long-term, the stadium will become an attraction for students and surrounding communities through its three world-class sporting pavilions: one for swimming, one for indoor sports, and a final pavilion for a track and field stadium. Training pitches and other sporting facilities radiate outwards from the stadium, all within easy reach of the local academic community.

The stadium is a clear demonstration of Qatar's commitment to sustainability and will focus on promoting a green, healthy future for all of us.
