CULTURAL SIGNIFICANCE

Al Janoub Stadium is a football stadium in Al-Wakrah, which is one of Qatar's oldest continuously inhabited areas and famous for pearl diving and fishing. To reflect the maritime heritage of its location, especially the traditional boat of the region – the dhow, the design incorporates cultural references in an abstracted manner and combines them with practical responses to the climate, context and the functional requirements of a football stadium.

The stadium’s roof design is an abstraction of the hulls of dhows turned upside-down and huddled together to provide shade and shelter. This is expressed in the stadium’s envelope geometry, details and selected materiality, including the roof’s beam structure that echoes the interior structure of a dhow’s hull.

The facades of the stadium are slanted outwards, tapered in elevation and reminiscent of dhow’s sail. The image of the dhow is further emphasized through the large overhang of the stadium’s eaves that incorporates strips of metal cladding echoing the timber structures used in a dhow.

SUSTAINABILITY

Al Janoub Stadium has achieved three sustainability certifications, which are GSAS Design & Build Certificate, GSAS Construction Management Certificate and Seasonal Energy Efficiency ratio certificate for energy center.

As well as the Al Bayt Stadium, the design also allows the Al Janoub Stadium to control the temperature with aerodynamic design. The roof provides significant shade, taking the weight off the advanced cooling systems.

Vast green spaces are being developed around the Al Janoub Stadium. This impressive parkland, with more than 800 new trees and low-water-consumption vegetation, will include space for horse riding, running tracks and cycle lanes. Improvements will also benefit Al Wakrah and Qatar far beyond 2022. Both by protecting the environment now and demonstrating how it can be done, Al Janoub Stadium will create a better future for generations to come.

TRANSPORTATION

By Metro

  • Take the red line on metro and stop at Al Wakrah Station
  • Take the free Metrolink buses from the station to Al Janoub Stadium.

  • Walk for 3 minutes from the Metrolink stop to Al Janoub stadium entrance

By Car/Taxi

Never in the modern history of the World Cup has the distance between the stadiums been so short. The close proximity of the stadiums to each other as well as other prominent places will allow you to enjoy the world cup matches as well as all the fun activities around the country.

Being the south-most stadium in the country, Al Janoub is

  • 30 km (Approx 27 min) from Education City Stadium
  • 42 km (Approx 42 min) from Lusail Stadium
  • 71 km (Approx 51 min) from Al Bayt Stadium
  • 22 km (Approx 24 min) from Souq Waqif
  • 28 km (Approx 28 min) from Corniche
  • 34 km (Approx 32 min) from Katara
  • 36 km (Approx 31 min) from Mall of Qatar
  • 38 km (Approx 34 min) from The Pearl
